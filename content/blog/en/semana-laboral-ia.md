## Will the 4-Day Workweek Arrive in 2040? AI Makes It Possible

A ChatGPT experiment suggests that advanced task automation will allow the four-day work week to be established globally by 2040, although sectors such as education and logistics could maintain five days due to operational requirements. [Source: Inc.com](https://www.inc.com)

This prediction is supported by studies that show how AI increases productivity by up to 40% in routine tasks, freeing up time for innovation and continuous training. [Source: SHRM](https://www.shrm.org)

---
## Projection Fundamentals

AI tools like Copilot and Gemini Code Assist can automate up to 30% of repetitive code, while intelligent management systems optimize administrative workflows. [Source: Yahoo Finance](https://es.finance.yahoo.com)

By integrating these improvements, companies would experience a reduction in working hours without sacrificing results.

---
## Examples and Pilots in Progress

- **Study in Iceland and Belgium**: Four-day trials have shown increases in satisfaction and efficiency, with AI managing automatic reassignment of non-critical tasks. [Source: BusinessThink](https://businessthink.unsw.edu.au)

- **SHRM Proposal**: The Society for Human Resource Management advocates for hybrid work frameworks, where AI evaluates workloads and reconfigures schedules in real time. [Source: SHRM](https://www.shrm.org)

---
## Challenges and Considerations

- **Sector inequality**: In-person jobs (health, logistics) will face barriers to reduce working days. [Source: Business Insider](https://www.businessinsider.com)

- **Ethical requirements**: Guarantee that productivity benefits translate into quality of life and not into higher performance expectations in less time.

- **Legislative adaptation**: Need to update labor standards to regulate flexible working hours and protect workers' rights.
