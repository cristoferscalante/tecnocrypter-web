# The UNESCO initiative at VivaTech 2026 and the urgency of migrating to quantum cryptography

During the celebration of the European technological summit **VivaTech 2026**, UNESCO has launched an important international initiative aimed at democratizing quantum development and preventing this technology from opening an unbridgeable gap between the global north and south. However, corporate cybersecurity experts present at the event have taken advantage of the forum to issue an urgent warning: the global migration towards **post-quantum cryptography (PQC)** must begin immediately.

Quantum danger is not a distant science fiction hypothesis. The offensive paradigm known as **"SNDL" (Store Now, Decrypt Later)** is an active reality: criminal groups and government agencies are capturing and storing encrypted data traffic in transit today, with the certainty that they will be able to decrypt it in a few years using commercial quantum computers.

### PQC Algorithms: The New Shielding Standards

Faced with the quantum threat to traditional RSA and elliptic curve (ECC) standards, NIST and global agencies have standardized algorithms based on multidimensional mathematical networks resistant to quantum attacks:
1. **Kyber (ML-KEM):** The leading standard for general encryption and secure cryptographic session key exchange.
2. **Dilithium (ML-DSA):** The recommended standard for advanced digital signatures, guaranteeing the integrity of documents and web certificates.

### Critical Steps for Enterprise Crypto Migration

* **Cryptographic Inventory:** Identify which databases, passwords, communication tunnels and APIs in your organization use vulnerable encryption at the infrastructure level.
* **Hybrid Adoption:** Configure firewalls and VPN networks to operate in a hybrid model, combining classical and post-quantum algorithms simultaneously.
