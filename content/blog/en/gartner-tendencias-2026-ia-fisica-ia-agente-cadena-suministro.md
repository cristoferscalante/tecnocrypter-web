# Gartner Reveals 2026 Technology Trends: The Rise of Physical AI and Agent AI

Global technology analysis has taken a definitive turn toward operational pragmatism at the end of June 2026. International consulting firm **Gartner** has published its guidelines on leading supply chain trends, positioning **Physical AI** and **Agent AI** as the two dominant technological forces that will redefine corporate productivity in the coming years.

The adoption of linguistic and generative models on screens has been only the prelude. In 2026, the real revolution lies in providing intelligence and interactivity to physical systems and automated transactional flows.

### Physical AI: The Brain Inside the Machine

Physical AI represents the natural evolution of industrial robotics. Instead of operating through rigid scripts of pre-calculated motion, robots and distribution arms in modern warehouses use multimodal models to:
1. **Recognize and adapt:** Identify damaged packages, poorly stowed merchandise, or sudden changes in the factory environment without stopping production.
2. **Real-time reinforcement learning:** Optimize packaging and distribution patterns based on direct dynamic simulations.

### AI Agent: The Autonomy of Administrative Processes

On the other hand, agent AI takes on decision-making tasks that previously required supervision by purchasing or logistics analysts. An integrated AI agent can:
* Monitor in real time climate or geopolitical alerts that may delay the delivery of supplies.
* Autonomously calculate optimal transportation detours.
* Interact with supplier APIs to issue emergency purchase orders under pre-established financial limits.

This transition to autonomous workflows requires that the integration software used by companies meet the highest standards of defensive design and logical auditing to avoid catastrophic failures.
